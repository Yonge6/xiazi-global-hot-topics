World CupNO.0106.20

All three hosts won within 24 hours, with Mexico and the U.S. advancing early; home advantage is not just noise, but the ability to turn pressure into wins.

From 05:00 on June 19 to 05:00 on June 20 Beijing time, three World Cup matches finished: Canada beat Qatar 6:0, Mexico beat South Korea 1:0, and the United States beat Australia 2:0. Canada earned its first men’s World Cup win in Vancouver as Jonathan David scored a hat trick. Mexico won a second straight match through Luis Romo’s goal and secured top spot in Group A and a knockout berth. The U.S., playing without Christian Pulisic, advanced to the last 32 after an Australian own goal and a header from 21-year-old Alex Freeman. The three co-hosts won in the same 24-hour window, scored nine goals in total and kept three clean sheets. As the group stage moves into its second round, depth under injuries and pressure is starting to matter as much as star power.

Middle EastNO.0206.20

Israel and Hezbollah restored a ceasefire, while U.S.-Iran talks were postponed; a ceasefire is not the end of peace, only the minimum condition for returning to the table.

Israel and Hezbollah agreed to restore a ceasefire after intense exchanges. Lebanon’s health ministry said Israeli strikes killed 47 people and wounded 97, while Israel said four soldiers were killed. EbolaNO.0306.20

Congo’s Ebola outbreak rose to 933 confirmed cases and 245 deaths; an outbreak becomes most dangerous when tracing falls behind transmission.

Congo’s health minister said the current Ebola outbreak has reached 933 confirmed cases, including 245 deaths, while 80 patients have recovered. The outbreak was first detected in eastern Ituri province, where population movement, limited health infrastructure and community resistance to testing mean the real scale may still not be fully visible.

EU-China TradeNO.0406.20

The EU is preparing a law to make firms diversify critical supplies; the next supply-chain contest is not who buys cheapest, but who avoids a single point of failure.

The European Commission is preparing a new supply-chain diversification law that would require companies to reduce dependence on a single country for critical materials and products. EU leaders say companies are moving too slowly to reduce supply risk, while the bloc’s goods trade deficit with China is nearing 1 billion euros a day. Concentrated supplies of critical minerals and industrial components are increasingly treated as an economic security issue.

AI Supply ChainNO.0506.20

China tightened indium export checks, raising concerns among global buyers; the AI race is not only about models and chips, but every rare metal behind optical chips.

Some overseas buyers said Chinese customs is asking for more detailed end-user information for indium products, with some approvals stretching from same-day processing to several days. China produces nearly 70% of the world’s indium, while indium phosphide is important for high-speed optical communication chips used in next-generation AI data centers. No formal export halt has been reported, but buyers are already reassessing supply risk.

AI EducationNO.0606.20

Norway will largely ban generative AI for elementary-school students; real AI education starts by ensuring children learn to think before asking tools to finish work.

Norway said that from the new school year, students aged 6 to 13 should generally not use generative AI in school. Students aged 14 to 16 may use it cautiously under teacher supervision, while those aged 17 to 19 will be taught how to use AI properly. The country also plans to increase paper books and handwriting instruction and reduce overreliance on tablets in classrooms.

Trump said he no longer views Anthropic as a national security threat; the stronger the model, the more the launch button looks like a diplomatic button.

AI TalentNO.0806.20

AlphaFold co-creator John Jumper is leaving Google DeepMind for Anthropic; top talent often chooses not the biggest organization, but the one closest to the mission.

John Jumper, the 2024 Nobel chemistry laureate and co-creator of AlphaFold, announced that he is joining Anthropic after nearly nine years at Google DeepMind. AlphaFold has predicted more than 200 million protein structures and sharply shortened biological and medical research cycles. The move comes days after Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer also left Google for OpenAI.

History and JusticeNO.0906.20

African and Caribbean states backed a 19-point slavery reparations plan; history cannot be rewritten, but responsibility can move from memory into institutions.

The African Union and Caribbean Community backed a unified reparations plan at a meeting in Ghana. It calls for formal apologies from countries involved in the transatlantic slave trade, a global reparations fund, debt relief, return of looted artifacts, climate justice funding and pathways for people of African descent to return and gain citizenship. The plan will be submitted to the next United Nations General Assembly.
