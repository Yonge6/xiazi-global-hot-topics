World CupNO.0106.21

The Netherlands beat Sweden 5-1 and Germany edged Côte d’Ivoire 2-1；as the second round begins, the real gap is not one burst of form but repeatable output。

From 05:00 June 20 to 05:00 June 21 Beijing time, five World Cup matches finished: Scotland 0-1 Morocco, Brazil 3-0 Haiti, Türkiye 0-1 Paraguay, Netherlands 5-1 Sweden, and Germany 2-1 Côte d’Ivoire. The Netherlands, held 2-2 by Japan in the opener, responded by beating a Sweden side that had opened with a 5-1 win over Tunisia. Germany followed its 7-1 rout of Curaçao with a narrower win, showing it can manage tougher games too. In the second round, contenders must prove not only star power, but a second answer after opponents study them.

U.S.-Iran TalksNO.0206.21

U.S. and Iranian delegations reached Switzerland as Iran claimed the Strait of Hormuz was closed；before talks even begin, both sides are fighting over who gets to define reality。

Senior U.S. and Iranian delegations prepared to start peace talks in Switzerland to implement a 60-day interim ceasefire. At the same time, Iran’s Revolutionary Guard said the Strait of Hormuz was closed, while U.S. Central Command disputed the claim and said 55 merchant ships carrying more than 17 million barrels of oil still passed that day. Continued fighting around Lebanon is also testing whether the agreement can become more than paper.

UK PoliticsNO.0306.21

Reports said Starmer could announce his resignation Monday, while government sources denied it；the real sign power is loosening is that everyone starts discussing the successor。

Britain’s Observer reported that Prime Minister Keir Starmer could announce his resignation on Monday and set out a timetable for departure. A government source said he remained focused on governing and no official resignation had been announced. More than 100 Labour MPs had reportedly called for him to step down, while potential challenger Andy Burnham’s return to parliament sharpened leadership speculation. This remains a major political report without official confirmation.

Ukraine WarNO.0406.21

Ukraine warned of a major Russian attack while striking 2,000 km away with drones；the depth of war is being redefined by long-range technology。

President Volodymyr Zelenskyy warned that Russia was preparing another large-scale attack and urged Ukrainians to heed air alerts. He also confirmed Ukrainian drones struck refining facilities in Russia’s Tyumen region in western Siberia, more than 2,000 km from Ukraine, and said new Ukrainian long-range drones can reach 3,000 km. Russia said the attack was repelled and initial reports showed no facility damage.

European HeatwaveNO.0506.21

Temperatures across Europe neared 41C as France restricted drinking at public events；extreme weather is moving from forecast to immediate city policy。

A new European heatwave intensified. France expected 35 departments to enter the highest red alert, with some areas possibly reaching 41C, prompting restrictions on alcohol consumption at music festivals and other public events, while Paris kept some parks open overnight. Germany issued broad heat warnings, and Spain closed a World Cup fan zone in Madrid.

Ebola OutbreakNO.0606.21

DR Congo Ebola cases rose to 956 with 247 deaths；before an outbreak truly loses control, crowding, water shortages and tracing failures usually appear first。

DR Congo’s latest figures showed confirmed Ebola cases rising from 933 to 956, with deaths increasing from 245 to 247. In a northeastern camp housing more than 15,000 displaced people, at least 30 people have died since May. Crowding, poor sanitation and resistance to testing are increasing transmission risks. The World Health Organization has treated the outbreak as an international public-health emergency.

Apple Supply ChainNO.0706.21

India is probing pollution complaints around Tata’s iPhone parts factory；moving manufacturing from one country to another does not make environmental costs disappear。

Indian health authorities are investigating Tata Electronics’ iPhone components plant in Tamil Nadu. Nearby farmers said factory discharge affected farmland and water sources, and some reported skin problems. Government tests on some water samples found E. coli and total dissolved solids above recommended drinking-water standards. Tata said internal tests found no contamination, and the probe is continuing.

Workplace MonitoringNO.0806.21

Canada’s TD Bank will track some employees’ browser and meeting time；remote work brought freedom, and also turned online status into a new timesheet。

Toronto-Dominion Bank told some employees in financial-crime and risk-management teams it will use WorkiQ software to track time spent on browsers, internal chat and meeting apps. The bank said the tool does not listen to conversations, read spreadsheets or use artificial intelligence, and is meant to improve staffing and workflow. Employees raised concerns over privacy, consent and future data use.

Child Online SafetyNO.0906.21

Meta lobbied Congress for protection from child-harm lawsuits；a platform may accept regulation without wanting to bear the consequences of past harm。

Sources and draft legislative text show Meta has lobbied the U.S. Congress to add language to the Kids Online Safety Act that would limit child-harm lawsuits brought under state law. Meta says it wants a uniform national standard rather than broad immunity. Critics argue the language could affect thousands of lawsuits already filed by young users, parents and schools. Lawmakers said they would not accept the disclosed immunity wording.
